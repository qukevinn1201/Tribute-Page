Lee "Faker" Sang-hyeok

The Unkillable Demon King

Timeline of SKT T1 Lee "Faker" Sang-hyeok

  • 1996 - Born in Seoul, Korea

  • 2011 - Disovered a popular MOBA called League of Legends and adapted to the game quickly

  • 2013 - Signs with SK Telecom T1 K as staring mid laner, later guiding T1 to win the 2013League of Legends World Championship.

  • 2014 - Did not qualify for worlds due to the organization having their talents split into two teams, this led to SKT T1 K and SKT T1 merging to create SKT T1.

  • 2015 - SKT T1 returns to the national scene with Faker competing for the starting mid lane role with Lee "Easyhoon" Ji-hoon as a sub. Their team would then go on to win the 2015 World Champhionship only acquring one defeat having a final score of 15-1.

  • 2016 - SKT T1 places first in 2016 MSI and third in 2016 Summer Split but in the end gains their third World Championship in 2016, Faker recieves MVP during this tournament.

  • 2017 - SKT T1 wins first in LCK Spring Split and first in MSI however, their winstreak was cut short by SSG in the 2017 World Championship.

  • 2018 - SKT T1 fails to qualify for Worlds in a very long time

  • 2019 - SKT T1 get eliminated during the semifainls of the 2019 World Championship

  • 2020 - SKT T1 did not qualify yet again for Worlds, however has had a major roster change, faker still remains as their starting mid laner

  • 2021 - SKT T1 make it to Worlds as the third seed with rookies on his team, T1 goes 2-3 agaisnt championship favorites Damwon Kia. Despite the loss, their journey from 2020-2021 had been a great one and hope that will will come back stronger.
